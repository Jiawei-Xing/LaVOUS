Supplementary Fig. X. ELBO approximation quality assessed by overdispersed-q importance-sampling marginal likelihood estimates. Panels A and B compare the saved variational negative ELBOs with IS-estimated full marginal negative log likelihoods for H0 and H1, respectively, using a stratified subset of 40 genes from the `t5r5` simulation. Because the original differential-test run used `const=false`, the gene-wise negative-binomial observation constant was added back to the saved ELBO losses before these absolute NLL comparisons. Panel C compares the ELBO-based likelihood-ratio statistic, `2*(H0 - H1)`, with the corresponding IS-estimated full LR. The red dotted line marks the ELBO LR cutoff corresponding to q = 0.05 in the full `t5r5` chi-square-calibrated differential-test result. Panel D compares mean absolute error for H0 NLL, H1 NLL, all LR values, and the LR subset with q > 0.05.

Importance sampling used an overdispersed-q proposal, `0.80 q(z|x) + 0.20 q_2(z|x)`, where q_2 has the same mean as q and 2 times the posterior standard deviation, with 3 independent replicates of S = 512 samples per gene and hypothesis (1536 pooled samples total). The fitted variational distributions, OU parameters, and NB dispersions were read from `expression_simulation/diff/`; no model was re-optimized and no original result files were modified. Summary metrics for this subset: Pearson r = 0.988 for ELBO LR versus IS full LR; mean absolute errors are H0 NLL = 13.419, H1 NLL = 10.226, LR = 6.565, and LR among q > 0.05 genes = 1.103 (n = 6); median replicate-based LR Monte Carlo SE = 3.013.
